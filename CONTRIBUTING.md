# Contributing

Thanks for helping keep this list accurate and useful.

## What belongs here

- Products that offer a **perpetual free tier**: an ongoing $0 plan (or equivalent) that does **not** expire after 7, 14, or 30 days.
- Actively maintained products or open-source projects — link to the **official** site or primary GitHub repository.
- **Honest limits**: if the free tier is small, say so in the table columns (limitations and paywall), do not hide caps.

## What does not belong

- **Time-limited free trials** that convert to paid with no continuing free option.
- “Free” offers that are only a marketing funnel with no real ongoing free use.
- Affiliate or referral links — use **canonical product URLs** only.
- Dead products or removed free tiers (open an issue first if you think something is historically important).

## Edge cases

| Situation | Policy |
|-----------|--------|
| Freemium with caps (messages, credits, models) | **Include** — describe caps under limitations. |
| Free for self-host / open source only | **Include** with a clear note that hosted unlimited use requires your own infrastructure. |
| Free API with rate limits | **Include** if it is indefinite at $0 for that tier. |
| Student / nonprofit-only free | **Include** with an **eligibility required** note. |

## Pull requests

1. Add your entry in **alphabetical order** within the correct category (or propose a new category in the PR description).
2. Use the same table columns as the README: Tool (markdown link), What it does, What’s free (forever), Main limitation, When you hit the paywall.
3. If you are **affiliated** with the product, say so in the PR.
4. Fix broken links you notice while editing.
5. Mention **what you verified** (e.g. pricing page date, in-app cap) in the PR body.

## What we skip

- Trial-only products presented as “free forever.”
- Hype or superlatives in descriptions — keep “What it does” to one neutral sentence.
